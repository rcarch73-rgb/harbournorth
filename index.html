      Projection controls

      Income and spending are entered in current-year dollars. Tax brackets, personal amounts, income, and spending are indexed using the projection inflation assumption.

      Year-by-year retirement projection

      Taxes are estimated separately for each person. Withdrawals cover the remaining after-tax cash-flow deficit, and taxable RRSP/RRIF withdrawals are included in the tax estimate.

      Explain this year

      Choose a year to see the main reasons behind its result.

      Current tax-engine rules

      • Federal and 2026 tax brackets for all 10 provinces and three territories are used as the base and indexed into future years using the projection inflation assumption.
      • Tax is estimated separately for each person using taxable recurring income and taxable RRSP/RRIF withdrawals.
      • Income sources marked non-taxable are excluded from taxable income. TFSA and cash withdrawals are not taxable.
      • The estimate includes federal and provincial or territorial basic personal credits, the Quebec federal abatement, B.C. tax reduction, and Ontario surtax, tax reduction, and health premium. It does not yet include CPP/EI/QPP payroll deductions, age and pension credits, OAS recovery tax, dividend gross-up, capital gains, medical credits, or income splitting.
      • Inheritance and gifts are treated as non-taxable cash flow. A generic one-time “Income” event is treated as taxable income for the primary person.
      • Deficits are funded in this order: non-registered, cash, TFSA, RRSP/RRIF, FHSA. RRSP/RRIF tax is recalculated until the after-tax shortfall stabilizes.
      • This is a planning estimate, not a tax return or professional tax advice.
